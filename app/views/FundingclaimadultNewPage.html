Submitting Evidence for Exceptional Adjustments

If you’ve included any exceptional adjustments in your final funding claim, you must send supporting evidence within 5 working days.

Please submit your evidence via the Home - Customer Help Portal, and clearly state that it relates to your 2024 to 2025 final funding claim.

If you're submitting evidence for more than one adjustment, make sure it’s clear which evidence supports each adjustment. If this isn’t clear, it could delay the review of your case.

Your case will be reviewed, and the outcome will be shown in your final reconciliation statement.

What to Include in Your Evidence

Please provide:

Why the adjustment was needed

Explain the issue and why you made the adjustment.

Proof of the error and its impact

Include learner-level details like:

  • Unique Learner Number (ULN) or student reference numbers
  • Cost and/or spending details to support your claim

Do not include personal data (for example, names, dates of birth, or addresses) to comply with GDPR.

Any extra information

Add anything else that supports your case.

Confirmation of authorisation

Confirm whether you had approval to include the adjustment. As stated in the 2024–2025 funding claims guidance:

"You must only use the ‘exceptional adjustments’ column in the final claim to make changes if you’ve agreed an exceptional adjustment with us beforehand. You must also retain relevant evidence (that we may request to validate your claim). Any unauthorised exceptional adjustments will not be included in your delivery figures."

Important

! Warning If you don’t send your evidence within 5 working days, your adjustment will be removed and won’t appear in your final reconciliation statement.
