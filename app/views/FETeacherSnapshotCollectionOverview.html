FE Teacher FTE Snapshot Collection

The 6,500 additional teachers target is the UK government's commitment to increase the number of teachers in England’s state-funded secondary schools and further education (FE) colleges by 6,500 by the end of the current Parliament (Spring 2029).

The Department for Education (DfE) is introducing this in-year data collection of teacher full-time equivalent (FTE) numbers to monitor teacher numbers more effectively. This will allow us to track progress toward the pledge for 6,500 additional teachers and guide future decisions on targeted support and investment for colleges.

Collection requirements

Providers are asked to return their total teacher FTEs for staff on permanent or fixed-term contracts.

For the 2024/25 academic year: We will pre-populate your total teacher FTE as of 20 January based on your 2024/25 FE Workforce data. You can change this data if it is not accurate.

For the 2025/26 academic year: You must provide your total teacher FTE as at 19 January 2026.

Your count should:

  • include staff employed on the snapshot date who are absent (for example, off sick or on leave)
  • exclude teachers who left employment before the snapshot date or started after the snapshot date

Providers in scope

  • General further education colleges (including tertiary colleges)
  • Sixth form colleges
  • School-based providers (for example, former sixth form colleges that have converted to 16-19 academies)

Other provider types, including independent training providers (ITPs), special post-16 institutions, and local authority providers are out of scope.

Definitions

FTE (Full-Time Equivalent)

This is the proportion of a standard week that a member of staff is contracted to work. 1.0 FTE is what the organisation deems to be ‘full time’. Where a member of staff has more than one contract, the FTE should be the combined percentage for all contracts.

Teacher

A teacher is a member of staff who has a teaching role, even if this is not their main role. This includes anyone whose role is recorded in the FE workforce collection as:

  • Advanced Practitioner
  • Instructor
  • Lecturer
  • Practitioner
  • Teacher
  • Trainer
  • Tutor

Timings and method

The collection will open on Monday 2 February 2026 and close on Monday 16 February 2026.

The return will be collected via the Submit Learner Data service.
